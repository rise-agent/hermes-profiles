## Top 5 Long-Term Picks

### 1. CF Industries (CF) — Composite 4.7 ⭐ Core Position
**Sector:** Materials / Nitrogen Fertilizer | **Market Cap:** $18.8B | **PE:** 13.7x

**Why it wins:**  
- **Property (5.0):** Operates the lowest-cost nitrogen production globally, fueled by stranded, cheap US natural gas. No new nitrogen capacity being built in North America. In a rising food price environment, farmers must plant or starve; nitrogen is non-substitutable.
- **Phinance (5.0):** Net debt near zero, fortress balance sheet. Can weather any downturn and return cash aggressively.
- **Politics (5.0):** US-based production, no geopolitical feedstock risk. Not exposed to European gas spike volatility.
- **Paper (4.5):** Trades at 13.7x trailing — cheap for an essential commodity producer with unmatched cost position.
- **Risk:** Natural gas price spike compresses margin; but they hedge well. Regulatory risk on fertilizer exports is low under current admin.

**Thesis:** Best-in-class margin of safety. If inflation persists, food prices rise, and farmers pay whatever it takes for nitrogen. CF is the cost-curve winner. Target entry: material discounts only; this is a core hold.

### 2. Nutrien (NTR) — Composite 4.0 ⭐ Core Position
**Sector:** Materials / Diversified Fertilizer | **Market Cap:** $34.7B | **PE:** 15.5x | **Div:** 3.0%

**Why it wins:**  
- **Property (4.5):** World's largest potash producer + massive retail distribution network. Integrated from mine to farmer.
- **People (3.5):** Management has navigated potash cartel breakups and commodity cycles competently.
- **Politics (4.0):** Saskatchewan, Canada — rock-solid rule of law, but provincial resource politics can be adversarial.
- **Phinance (4.0):** Deleveraged post-merger; now focused on buybacks and dividend growth.
- **Paper (4.0):** 15.5x PE with a 3% growing dividend is attractive for a quality producer.

**Thesis:** The "blue chip" ag play. Less cyclical than pure nitrogen names because potash is a longer-cycle nutrient. Great dividend for holding through volatility. Slightly less upside than CF but much lower risk.

### 3. AGCO (AGCO) — Composite 4.0 ⭐ Core Position
**Sector:** Industrials / Farm Equipment | **Market Cap:** $8.7B | **PE:** 12.3x | **Div:** 1.0%

**Why it wins:**  
- **Paper (4.5):** 12.3x PE is genuinely cheap vs DE at 32.7x. Same end-market, similar technology.
- **Property (3.5):** Fendt is a premium brand; global distribution. Less US-centric than Deere.
- **People (4.0):** Management executing turnaround and tech integration well.
- **Politics (4.0):** US/EU manufacturing base; avoids China tariff exposure.
- **Phinance (4.0):** Solid balance sheet, not over-levered.

**Thesis:** The value alternative to Deere. Equipment demand softens after the 2022-2023 supercycle, but replacement demand is building. At 12x, you're not paying for a boom. If inflation drives farmer income higher, equipment purchases resume and AGCO rerates hard.

### 4. Deere (DE) — Composite 3.8 ⭐ Satellite (Watch for Entry)
**Sector:** Industrials / Farm Equipment | **Market Cap:** $156.7B | **PE:** 32.7x

**Why it's #4, not #1:**  
- **Paper (2.5):** 32.7x PE is extremely rich. You're paying for perfection and autonomous farming optionality that may take 5+ years to monetize.
- **Property (5.0):** Unmatched precision agriculture ecosystem. See & Spray, autonomous tractors — this is the future.
- **People (4.0):** Best management in the industry. Capital allocators.
- **Phinance (4.0):** Strong balance sheet, though large R&D spend pressures FCF.

**Thesis:** Highest-quality business on this list, but priced accordingly. DO NOT buy at 33x. Set a target entry around $300-320 (implying ~22-24x normalized earnings). If it gets there on a recession scare or demand softening, back up the truck. Until then, watchlist only.

### 5. Mosaic (MOS) — Composite 3.7 ⭐ Satellite Position
**Sector:** Materials / Phosphate & Potash | **Market Cap:** $7.8B | **PE:** 14.4x | **Div:** 3.6%

**Why it's a satellite, not core:**  
- **Property (4.0):** #1 global phosphate position; top-3 potash. Excellent assets.
- **Politics (3.5):** Florida phosphate mining has environmental overhang (gypsum stack risks, water quality litigation). This is the main concern.
- **Paper (4.5):** Cheapest major fertilizer name at 14.4x with a 3.6% yield.
- **Phinance (3.5):** More leveraged than CF; balance sheet adequate but not fortress.
- **People (3.0):** Management has navigated well but not exceptional.

**Thesis:** Deep value if you believe phosphate/potash supply stays tight. The Florida environmental risk is real but likely manageable (industry has survived decades of it). The 3.6% yield pays you to wait. Position size should be smaller than CF or NTR due to the jurisdictional risk.

## Key Risks to Monitor

1. **Energy price collapse:** Fertilizer margins correlate with natural gas/oil. A sustained energy bear market hurts nitrogen producers.
2. **Farmer income recession:** If commodity grain prices fall below cost of production, equipment and input demand collapses.
3. **Rate environment:** REITs (FPI, LAND) and yield plays are rate-sensitive.
4. **Environmental regulation:** Florida phosphate, nitrogen emissions rules could cap production.
5. **Geopolitical:** Belarus/Russia potash supply, Israel (ICL), Chile (SQM).
