## The Risks: What Could Go Wrong

### 🔴 1. Permitting Delays (High Probability, Moderate Impact)

Casino is in the **Yukon Environmental and Socio-economic Assessment Act (YESAA)** process. This is slow — multi-year — and subject to:
- First Nations consultation (Selkirk First Nation)
- Federal + territorial environmental review
- Water management concerns (Casino is in the Yukon River watershed)

**Reality check:** The 2022 feasibility study was published. Environmental baseline studies are ongoing. A realistic timeline is **5-8 years to production** from today. If you're a 5-year holder, you may not see first pour. This is why the 12% discount rate is justified.

### 🔴 2. Financing Risk (Moderate Probability, High Impact)

Capex for Casino is estimated at **$2.8B+**. WRN's market cap is $1.2B. They cannot self-finance this. Options:
- Joint venture / farm-out to a major (most likely)
- Debt + equity raise (dilutive)
- Streaming/royalty sale (expensive)

If copper prices collapse during the financing window, the project becomes uneconomic and financing dries up. This is the **#1 value-destruction risk** for developers.

### 🔴 3. Commodity Price Risk (Ever-present)

Our $4.50/lb copper deck protects us. But if copper falls to $3.50 for a sustained period:
- Casino's margin collapses
- NAV falls to ~$5-6/share
- Stock likely drops to $1.50-2.00
- Project may be shelved indefinitely

**Mitigation:** WRN is an option on copper. Don't size it as a core position. 2% max.

### 🟡 4. Execution / Construction Risk (Moderate Probability)

WRN has never built a mine. Their CEO, Paul West-Sells, is a geologist — not an operator. If they retain 100% ownership and try to build themselves, execution risk is high. This is why a JV to a major is the bull-case path.

### 🟡 5. Share Dilution (High Probability, Low-Moderate Impact)

Developers burn cash on G&A + permitting. WRN will likely issue shares annually to fund these costs. At $2.76/share, that's painful. If the stock stays here, the share count could grow 20-30% over 3-5 years before production funding. This is already embedded in our NAV model (450M shares = diluted estimate).

### 🟡 6. Jurisdiction Risk — Canada Is Good, But Not Zero

Yukon is remote. Infrastructure (roads, power) is limited. The **Dawson Range** has seen mining for a century, but Casino's scale is unprecedented for the territory. There is no guarantee that the political/regulatory environment stays supportive for 5-8 years. A change in federal government could shift environmental review priorities.
